Thiruvalla is a vibrant town in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India. Known as the “Land of Non-Resident Indians” due to its large expatriate population, it is a hub of culture, spirituality, and commerce. The town is famous for its ancient temples, including the Sree Vallabha Temple, a significant Vaishnavite shrine with rich history and traditions.

Geography

  • The town is spread over an area of 27.15 km2 (10.48 sq mi) and has a population of 52,883, down from 56,837 in 2001. It lies on the northern banks of the Manimala River, in a land-locked region surrounded by irrigating canals and rivers..
  • Thiruvalla is also known as the "Town of Non-Resident Indians", as a large portion of its inhabitants form a part of the Malayali diaspora. Though Thiruvalla struggles with an aging and declining population and experiences a high emigration rate, in recent years the town has experienced growth in its economy and urban revitalisation..
